About The Weeknd

The Weeknd, born Abel Makkonen Tesfaye on February 16, 1990, in Toronto, Canada, is a globally acclaimed singer, songwriter, and record producer. Known for his distinctive voice and genre-blending music that combines R&B, pop, and hip-hop, The Weeknd has become one of the most influential artists of his generation. He first gained recognition with his mixtapes "House of Balloons," "Thursday," and "Echoes of Silence" in 2011, which showcased his dark, atmospheric sound and introspective lyrics. Over the years, he has released several successful albums, including "Beauty Behind the Madness," "Starboy," and "After Hours," featuring hit singles like "Can't Feel My Face," "Starboy," and "Blinding Lights." The Weeknd's music often explores themes of love, fame, and excess, resonating with a wide audience. Beyond his musical achievements, he has also made significant contributions to film soundtracks and has been recognized with numerous awards, including multiple Grammy Awards.
